If all investment returns were guaranteed, investors would simply invest all of their money in the asset with the highest return. But investment returns aren’t guaranteed. Investments respond to economic conditions in different ways. For example, during periods of inflation, real estate investments may return more than bonds with fixed interest rates. During recessions, bonds usually have higher returns than real estate.

Diversifying among several investment categories helps stabilize your portfolio’s return while minimizing its risk. It helps protect your portfolio from extreme market fluctuations.

As you go through your life, your risk tolerance level will change and so will the time frame for your investments. These changes will affect your desired investment mix. Here are examples of how the diversified composition of your portfolio might change over time.
